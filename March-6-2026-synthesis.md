### Designed Experiment: "Origami Flow Scaffold"

**Hypothesis:**
A waterbomb-base origami structure with nested petal folds will channel fluid flow along pre-determined paths determined solely by fold geometry — demonstrating that topology precedes and scripts dynamics.

**Materials:**
- Waterproof origami paper or thin mylar sheet
- Water + food dye (3 colors for 3 flow paths)
- Macro camera or phone on tripod
- Optional: small LED light source underneath for transparency

**Protocol:**

*Step 1 — Build the scaffold:*
Fold a waterbomb base. Add 4 petal folds at cardinal points. Add accordion stem below. The flower sits on the stem — the whole unit is one continuous surface.

*Step 2 — Map the topology first:*
Before any water — photograph and diagram the fold lines. Label: knots (crossing points), folds (crease lines), slip points (reversible sink folds), nested regions (petal interiors). This is your "topological blueprint."

*Step 3 — Flow test:*
Introduce dyed water at the top center (waterbomb cavity). Observe:
- Which paths does water follow? (Should match fold lines exactly)
- Where does it pool? (Nested knot regions — the "TAD" equivalent)
- Where does it release? (Slip fold points — the "cohesin loop" equivalent)
- Does the stem distribute evenly? (Fold gradient field test)

*Step 4 — Perturbation test:*
Collapse one sink fold manually (simulate mis-timed chromatin folding). Re-introduce water. Does the flow pattern break down? Does it reroute or stall?

*Step 5 — Document:*
- Before/after topology diagram
- Flow path video
- Perturbation comparison
- Map results to biological equivalents (TAD = waterbomb cavity, cohesin loop = sink fold, gene activation = petal opening sequence)

**Why this is the smoking gun:**
It's visual, replicable, zero-cost, and directly maps to the embryogenesis proof case. Same four primitives (loop, fold, slip, nested) operating identically in paper and in living DNA. Cross-domain demonstration in one afternoon.
